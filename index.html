Contraceptives to Prevent Unintended Pregnancy and Diseases

Objectives

  • Know different types of contraceptive and their importance
  • Understand how to properly use different types of contraceptives
  • Determine potential risks of each contraceptive method
  • Explain interventions in preventing STIs and unintended pregnancies using contraceptive methods

Importance

This is essential for preventing unintended pregnancies and STIs, empowering individuals to make informed reproductive choices and promoting overall public health.

Types of Contraceptives

Pills

  • A hormonal contraceptive
  • Taken daily (sometimes with a week break)
  • Very effective (over 99%) if taken correctly
  • Does not protect against STIs

Condom

  • A hormone-free contraceptive
  • Covers the penis during sex
  • Protects against STIs
  • 98% effective if used correctly

There are many more types of contraception. Consult a doctor before changing methods.

How to Properly Use Different Types of Contraceptives

Hormonal Method

These contraceptives work by altering hormone levels in the body to prevent pregnancy. Examples:

  • Birth control pills
  • Hormonal IUD
  • Patches
  • Shots
  • Implants

Non-Hormonal Method

Methods that create physical or chemical barriers to prevent sperm from fertilizing an egg. Examples:

  • Physical barrier methods
  • IUD
  • Spermicidal agents
  • Fertility awareness methods
  • Withdrawal method

Potential Risks of Contraceptive Methods

Condoms

  • Breakage or slippage
  • Latex allergies
  • Irritation
  • Decreased sensitivity
  • Incorrect use

Pills

  • Blood clots
  • High blood pressure
  • Weight gain and mood changes
  • Nausea and headaches
  • Liver issues (rare)
  • Missed pills

STIs (Sexually Transmitted Infections)

STIs can lead to serious health problems. Risk factors include:

  • Unprotected sex
  • Multiple partners
  • History of STIs
  • Injecting drugs

STIs are categorized into bacterial, viral, parasitic, and fungal types.

Dual Protection (Pregnancy + STI)

Dual protection involves using methods to prevent both pregnancy and STIs. The most common contraceptive barrier is the condom.
